01 About the day change

Is Mon/Wed boulder, Fri/Sat lead a problem?

Not the shape of it — you still get 2 boulder + 2 lead days, same logic as before, just moved to fit your week. The one thing I changed from what you described: you said Mon/Tue/Wed would all be at the boulder gym "doing all my exercises." If Tuesday means hard climbing too, that's 3 straight days of high-force finger loading, which is more than the usual 4-day/week ceiling and a real overuse-injury setup — especially given your finger history. So below, Tuesday stays at the boulder gym but is a no-climbing strength day (pull-ups, push-ups, legs, core, Tindeq check) — fingers get a break between Monday's limit day and Wednesday's 4×4 day, while you still only need to show up at one gym. If you'd genuinely rather climb Tuesday too, easy technique mileage only, no limit attempts and no 4×4, would be the least-risky version.

Why Friday = quality, Saturday = endurance?

Thursday (rest/legs/swim) leaves you freshest for Friday, so that's when you want full attempts on your project. Saturday, climbing with some residual fatigue from Friday, is actually the better day to train "keep going through the pump" — that's the specific quality endurance day is for.

Can I occasionally swap Wednesday's boulder with Saturday's lead?

Yes, and no, you don't need to rebuild anything for it — this is a one-week tactical swap, not a change to the underlying logic. The week is built as two blocks, each "fresh quality day → endurance day," and the swap only touches the two endurance slots (Wed 4×4 and Sat mileage) — Monday and Friday, your two quality days, are untouched either way. The one thing worth knowing: right now the more explosive session (boulder 4×4) sits in the safer slot — buffered by Tuesday's rest day — while the steadier one (lead mileage) sits in the slot with zero recovery, straight after Friday. Swap them and you're doing dynamic, powerful bouldering on already-tired fingers with no rest day behind it — exactly the combination that causes acute tweaks, especially with your finger history. So: swap occasionally without worry, just dial the 4×4 back that week (3 rounds instead of 4, easier problems) rather than running it at full intensity when it lands the day after a climbing session.

06 How the numbers progress

Repeat the week for three weeks, building load each week, then deload and retest on week 4.
WeekWhat changes
1Baseline — exactly as prescribed.
2+1 rep on push-up and squat sets. Bump the 4×4 problems up half a grade if you didn't reach a real pump by round 3 in week 1.
3+1.25–2.5 kg on weighted pull-ups if 4×5 was clean for two sessions running. Bump limit-bouldering attempts to the next grade if 2 of 3–4 problems went down.
4Deload — cut volume ~40% everywhere. Retest: Tindeq, pull-up max reps, weighted pull-up 5RM, push-up max, hardest clean boulder, hardest redpoint. Start the next block from the new numbers.

07 Your data & export

Everything you log is saved only in this browser, on this device — there's no account, no server, and nothing is sent anywhere, whether you open this from Claude or from a hosted link. That's true no matter where this page lives: it means clearing Safari's site data (or opening the link on a different phone, or a friend opening the same link) starts from a blank tracker. Use Export below to save a backup file whenever you like — that same file is what you'd hand to the real iPhone app once it exists.
Heavy/slow work (Tue's weighted pull-ups) builds max strength and tendon stiffness; light/incomplete-rest work (Wed's 4×4) builds power-endurance — the two don't substitute for each other, which is why the week trains both on purpose.
