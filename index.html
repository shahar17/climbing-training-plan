About the day layout

Is Mon/Wed boulder, Fri/Sat lead a problem?

Not the shape of it — 2 boulder + 2 lead either way. Tuesday stays at the boulder gym but is a no-climbing day, so fingers get a break between Monday's limit day and Wednesday's 4×4 — three straight days of hard finger loading was the one thing worth avoiding, especially with your history.

Isn't that 5+ training days? Hörst says cap it at 4.

It's 4 climbing days (Mon/Wed/Fri/Sat) plus Tuesday — but Tuesday only carries general conditioning (push-ups, dips, antagonist, legs, core), which Hörst's own book treats as fine on any day, including "twice per week" antagonist work regardless of climbing schedule. The two things that actually count as sport-specific training — weighted pull-ups and the Tindeq finger check — live on Monday now, inside one of your 4 climbing days, specifically so they don't add a 5th sport-specific day. Thursday's legs/swim option is the same story: general conditioning, not sport-specific, so it doesn't count against the cap either.

Do bouldering, the 4×4, and the hangboard each get their own "recovery budget"?

No — treat them as one shared pool for your fingers. This is the single biggest injury-prevention rule in the whole plan, especially given your pulley history: a hangboard set after a hard bouldering session isn't "light" just because it's a different exercise, since it's the same tendons and pulleys absorbing load either way. Practically: if Monday's limit attempts were genuinely hard, take Monday's Tindeq test or Wednesday's density hangs down a notch (bigger edge, fewer sets) rather than running them at the number written down. Sharp or localized pulley pain is the one signal that overrides everything else in this plan — stop and reduce load immediately, don't push through it.

Why Friday = quality, Saturday = endurance?

Thursday leaves you freshest for Friday's full project attempts. Saturday, with some residual fatigue from Friday, is the better day to train "keep going through the pump."

Can I occasionally swap Wednesday's boulder with Saturday's lead?

Yes, no rebuild needed — it only touches the two endurance slots, not the quality days. Just know the more explosive session (4×4) is normally buffered by Tuesday's rest; swapped onto Saturday it lands straight after a climbing day, so dial it back that week (3 rounds, easier problems) rather than pushing full intensity.

How the numbers progress

Repeat the week for 3 weeks building load, then deload + retest on week 4.
1Baseline — exactly as prescribed.
2+1 rep push-ups/squats. Bump 4×4 grade if no pump by round 3.
3Same pull-up rep count as week 1, add +1.25–2.5kg if last week's reps were clean. Bump the grade logged on Monday's limit attempts, Wednesday's 4×4, Friday's project, and Saturday's volume routes if they felt clearly sub-max.
4Deload ~40% volume. Retest Tindeq, pull-ups, push-ups, hardest send/redpoint.
